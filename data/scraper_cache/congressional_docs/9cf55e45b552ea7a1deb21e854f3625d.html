Tours and Tickets

Image

INFORMATION

The Washington, D.C. office can help answer questions you may have about a visit to our nation's capital and can place requests for your tour tickets to some of D.C.'s popular attractions and historical landmarks. Advance Congressional tour ticket availability varies throughout the year, so please fill out the form below with at least 8-12 weeks advance notice whenever possible.

Advance Congressional tour tickets are sometimes available for the following attractions:

  • U.S. Capitol
  • The White House
  • Bureau of Engraving & Printing
  • Library of Congress
  • Kennedy Center
  • FBI Education Center
  • Supreme Court
  • National Archives

Advance Congressional tour tickets are provided by the tour site on a first come, first served basis. Please be aware that even with significant advanced notice, tour requests for these attractions often fill up quickly, especially during the peak tourist season of February through August. You may receive confirmation of tour availability on a rolling basis, and oftentimes, right up until the date of your departure.

When you arrive in D.C., please feel free to stop by the office in the Longworth House Office Building – room 1401. Staff will be happy to provide you with a gallery pass to attend a session of the House of Representatives.

Whitehouse Dates

Please choose three dates everyone in your party is available to tour the White House. Tours are available Tuesday through Thursday 7:30 a.m. to 11:00 a.m., and Friday and Saturday from 7:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. The tours are self-guided.

White House tours may be submitted up to 6 months in advance, but no later than 3 weeks before the requested tour date.

Each person in your tour party must submit security information to the White House via a link they provide.
